Step right up, folks. The bleachers are full, the mustard is warm, and a question older than the seventh-inning stretch is about to be settled on the field of reason. Is a hot dog a sandwich? Three innings of evidence below.

★ Play Ball: The Three Inning Argument ★

1

WHAT EVEN IS A SANDWICH?

— Inning One · The Definition —
YES, IT IS

Bread + filling = sandwich

Merriam-Webster defines a sandwich as two or more slices of bread with a filling in between. A hot dog has bread.

Source · Merriam-Webster

The USDA agrees

The U.S. Department of Agriculture officially classifies a hot dog as a sandwich for regulatory purposes.

Source · USDA FSIS Guidelines

Earl of Sandwich logic

The Earl wanted to eat with one hand while playing cards. It is entirely possible to eat a hotdog with one hand.

Source · 1762, give or take
NO, IT IS NOT

The bun is HINGED

A real sandwich has two separate pieces of bread. A hot dog bun is one piece, folded.

Source · Structural Bread Theory

National Hot Dog Council ruling

In 2015, the National Hot Dog and Sausage Council issued a formal statement: a hot dog is its own category, "an exclamation of joy, a song of summer." Not a sandwich.

Source · NHDSC, July 2015

Linguistic common sense

Nobody at the ballpark says "I'd like a hot dog sandwich, please." If a word does its own work, the word stays.

Source · Common Usage

★ INNING TWO: THE CUBE RULE ★

A popular internet framework classifies foods by where the starch sits relative to the filling. By this rule, a hot dog is not a sandwich at all. It is, technically, a taco. Sit with that for a moment.

— BOTTOM OF THE NINTH —

FINAL RULING

After three full innings of arguments, the umpire calls it on the field: a hot dog is, by the common sense of the bleachers and the official word of the National Hot Dog and Sausage Council, not a sandwich. It is, however, definitely a taco. The federal government may appeal at any time.
