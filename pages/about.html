Background

Who I Am

Kayla Canfield is an Administrative Assistant II at Mount St. Mary's University, where she supports faculty and staff across the English, History, Communications, Political Science, and Sociology departments. She is completing her master's degree in Instructional Design and Technology, with a focus on applying learning design principles to higher education and professional development contexts.

Her undergraduate degree in Interdisciplinary Studies, combining English and Communications with a minor in History, shapes how she approaches design work. She brings a humanities perspective to instructional problems, which means she pays close attention to language, narrative, and the way people make meaning from information.

She has developed training materials, designed communication pieces, organized academic workflows, and supported events and projects across multiple departments. She also continues her work as a writer and researcher, with published and award-winning academic writing in American literature, cultural studies, and education.

Approach

How I Work

Good instructional design starts with listening. Before building anything, she works to understand what learners actually need, what context they are working in, and what success looks like for the people who will use the material.

She applies ADDIE and SAM frameworks, Universal Design for Learning principles, and clear information architecture to every project. She also brings her writing background into design work, because the quality of the language in a learning material matters as much as its structure.

She is drawn to projects that sit at the intersection of education, culture, and communication, and she is especially interested in how design can support first-generation students, adult learners, and people navigating complex institutional systems.
