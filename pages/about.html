Hello!

Kayla Canfield

Instructional Designer · Writer · Researcher · MEd Candidate

I am a master's student in Instructional Design and Technology at Mount St. Mary's University, where I also work as an Administrative Assistant II. I design learning materials, build workplace systems, and write about culture, history, and education. My work sits at the intersection of instructional design and the humanities, and I bring the same care to both.

I grew up reading everything I could find and writing about what I read. That habit shapes how I approach every project. I am drawn to work that asks me to think carefully about what someone needs to understand and how to help them get there.

FROM THE DESK OF: Kayla Canfield  —  RE: Who I Am 2024

Background

A designer with a humanities core

I am completing my master's degree in Instructional Design and Technology while working in higher education administration at Mount St. Mary's University. My undergraduate degree combined English, Communications, and History, and that background shapes how I approach every project I take on.

At MSMU, I support faculty and staff across multiple departments. The combination of graduate study and full-time work has given me a practical education in how institutions function, what learners need, and where communication tends to break down.

RE: What I Do Present

Practice

Design, writing, and administration

My day-to-day work includes designing instructional materials, managing faculty support across departments, coordinating academic events, and maintaining records systems. I also write, both for academic purposes and for my own research interests in American literature and cultural history.

I approach instructional design the same way I approach writing: with attention to structure, clarity, and the reader's experience. I am interested in how people learn, how institutions communicate, and how good design can make both easier.

RE: Where I Am Going 2025 — Forward

Goals

Instructional design roles and doctoral study

I am currently seeking instructional design positions in higher education, nonprofit, or corporate learning contexts. I am also applying to doctoral programs in humanities, education, and information science, where I hope to continue research on digital humanities, archival pedagogy, and the intersection of media and cultural memory.

I am drawn to programs and organizations that take both design and the humanities seriously, and that see them as complementary rather than competing.

View My Work Read My Writing

Scholarship

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of American literature, cultural history, and digital humanities. I am particularly interested in how communities preserve and transmit memory, and how those processes are shaped by media, technology, and institutional structures.

I am also interested in the pedagogy of writing and reading, and in how instructional design can draw on humanistic methods to create more meaningful learning experiences.
